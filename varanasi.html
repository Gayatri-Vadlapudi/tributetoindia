Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the world’s oldest continuously inhabited cities and a major spiritual and cultural center in India. Located on the banks of the Ganges River in Uttar Pradesh, Varanasi is revered in Hinduism as the holiest city, deeply connected with pilgrimage, rituals, and the quest for moksha (liberation from the cycle of life and death). The city is famous for its ghats series of steps leading down to the river where rituals, daily worship, and funerary rites take place. The Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, a nightly ceremony involving fire, music, and chanting, is a highlight for visitors and devotees. Two main cremation ghats, Manikarnika and Harishchandra, are considered among the most sacred places for Hindu final rites. Varanasi is also renowned for:Banarasi silk sarees and traditional crafts. A vibrant tradition in Indian classical music, dance, and arts, being the home or muse for legends like Ravi Shankar and Ustad Bismillah Khan. The city’s labyrinthine old quarters are filled with temples and shrines, with the Kashi Vishwanath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva being the most venerated. Varanasi’s spiritual significance extends into Buddhism and Jainism: Sarnath, just outside the city, is where Buddha delivered his first sermon, and the city is also a Jain pilgrimage site. Across millennia, Varanasi has weathered waves of invasions, religious changes, and colonial rule, yet its traditions, rituals, and aura of spirituality remain as intense and immersive as ever. Not just a religious capital, Varanasi is also important for its arts, education, cuisine, and its bustling, sensory-rich daily life making it both a spiritual destination and a living museum of Indian heritage.
